Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Life Insurance

The amount of coverage you need depends on your income, debts, future expenses like college tuition, and your family's lifestyle. A good rule of thumb is 10-12 times your annual income, but we'll do a detailed needs analysis to determine the right amount for your situation.

Term life provides coverage for a specific period (10-30 years) at a fixed premium. It's more affordable but has no cash value. Whole life provides lifelong coverage with premiums that never increase and builds cash value over time. The right choice depends on your budget and long-term goals.

Many policies require a simple medical exam (height, weight, blood pressure, blood/urine sample), but we also offer no-exam policies with accelerated underwriting. The exam helps secure the best rates for healthy individuals.

Yes! While pre-existing conditions may affect your rates, many carriers offer coverage options for various health situations. I work with multiple insurers to find the best solution for clients with health challenges.

Simplified issue policies can provide instant approval, while fully underwritten policies typically take 2-6 weeks depending on medical records and exam scheduling. I'll guide you through every step to make the process as quick and smooth as possible.
